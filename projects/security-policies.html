Security Policies with Control Mappings

Overview

Two complete security policies—Acceptable Use and Incident Response—drafted with CMMC Level 2 requirements in mind. Each policy statement is mapped to specific NIST 800-53 controls and CMMC practices.

Purpose: Demonstrate ability to draft enforceable policies, understand policy structure, and map documentation to compliance frameworks.

Artifacts Included

  • Acceptable Use Policy: 2 pages covering authorized use, prohibited activities, monitoring, and enforcement
  • Incident Response Policy: 2 pages covering roles, phases (preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, post-mortem), and reporting requirements
  • Control Mapping Matrix: Each policy statement mapped to NIST 800-53 and CMMC Level 2 practices
  • Policy Formatting: Version control, approval fields, and review dates included

Policy Example

Incident Response Policy Statement:

"All suspected security incidents must be reported to the Incident Response Team within 1 hour of discovery. The IRT will triage, classify, and respond according to the severity matrix defined in the Incident Response Plan."

Control Mapping: NIST 800-53: IR-4 (Incident Handling) · CMMC: IR.2.092

Frameworks & Controls

NIST 800-53: IR-4, IR-6, AC-8, PS-6

CMMC Level 2: IR.2.092, IR.2.093, AC.2.010, PS.2.054
