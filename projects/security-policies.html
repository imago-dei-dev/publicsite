Version: 1.0 Effective Date: March 2026 Review Date: March 2027 Approved By: CISO (Mock)

Policy Overview

Two complete security policies drafted for a mock logistics company. Each policy is mapped to NIST 800-53 controls and CMMC Level 2 practices—demonstrating my ability to write enforceable policies that satisfy compliance requirements.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Defines authorized use of company systems, prohibited activities, monitoring rights, and enforcement.
  • Incident Response Policy (IRP): Establishes roles, responsibilities, and procedures for detecting, containing, and recovering from security incidents.

Policy Statement + Control Mapping

Acceptable Use Policy

Statement 1: Authorized Use

"Company information systems are provided for business purposes related to logistics operations, customer support, and internal communications. Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with business operations, consume excessive resources, or violate any other policy."

NIST 800-53: AC-1 (Access Control Policy), AC-2 (Account Management)
CMMC Level 2: AC.1.001 (Limit information system access), AC.2.007 (Employ least privilege)

Statement 2: Prohibited Activities

"The following activities are strictly prohibited: accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content; attempting to bypass security controls; sharing credentials; installing unapproved software; and any activity that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company systems or CUI."

NIST 800-53: CM-6 (Configuration Settings), SI-4 (System Monitoring)
CMMC Level 2: CM.2.056 (Security configuration), SI.2.043 (System monitoring)

Statement 3: Monitoring and Enforcement

"The company reserves the right to monitor all use of information systems to ensure compliance with this policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and referral for criminal prosecution where applicable."

NIST 800-53: AU-2 (Audit Events), PS-6 (Access Agreements)
CMMC Level 2: AU.2.041 (Audit events), PS.2.054 (Access agreements)

Incident Response Policy

Statement 1: Reporting Requirements

"All suspected security incidents must be reported to the Incident Response Team within 1 hour of discovery. Reports can be made via phone, email, or the designated incident reporting portal. Failure to report known incidents is a violation of this policy."

NIST 800-53: IR-4 (Incident Handling), IR-6 (Incident Reporting)
CMMC Level 2: IR.2.092 (Incident response capability), IR.2.093 (Incident reporting)

Statement 2: Incident Response Phases

"All incidents shall be managed according to the following phases: 1) Preparation, 2) Detection & Analysis, 3) Containment, Eradication & Recovery, 4) Post-Incident Activity. The Incident Response Lead shall document each phase in the incident report."

NIST 800-53: IR-2 (Incident Response Training), IR-5 (Incident Monitoring)
CMMC Level 2: IR.2.094 (Incident response training), IR.2.095 (Incident monitoring)

Policy Hierarchy (Policy vs. Standard vs. Procedure)

Policy: High-level, enforceable rules (what we do and why)
Standard: Specific requirements and configurations (how we meet the policy)
Procedure: Step-by-step instructions (who does what and when)

These policies are supported by technical standards (e.g., NIST 800-171 configuration baselines) and detailed procedures (e.g., incident response playbooks) that are maintained separately.
